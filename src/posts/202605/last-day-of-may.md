## Last Day of May

Well, its the last day of may and I am still learning [Svelte](https://svelte.dev/) and [Typescript](https://www.typescriptlang.org/) and building functions. This whole site has been built in May (since May 9th in fact) and even in the last 48 hours I have moved the visual styles to be based on [TailwindCSS](https://tailwindcss.com/) and [DaisyUI](https://daisyui.com/).

A few things I have learnt in the last month,

1. [TypeScript](https://www.typescriptlang.org/) is as good as most languages. I have only done small quantities of JavaScript, but I have to say that I like [TypeScript](https://www.typescriptlang.org/) much better than I recall liking JavaScript. It might be because of the volume of Python I am doing lately. 
1. Browser niceness and consistency is much better than it was 20 years ago. I remember spending most of my time getting simple formatting or functions working across the browsers and by the time something simple was working all of my energy was sapped. 
1. [Railway.com](http://railway.com/) is great. I did not look far and wide when I made my choice for [Railway.com](http://railway.com/) but I am now a paying customer.
1. [Redis](https://redis.io/) is simple & great. An awesome combo. 
1. Large Language Models are very good at pumping out this stuff and finding issues in structure. I have been using [Claude](https://claude.ai/) for a lot of my learning here and its much quicker than bouncing around Google search looking for examples. 
1. This tool chain is just a productivity machine. I am used to waiting for app servers to boot and having relatively slow cycles in my iterations. I am not used to this at all for web development and I really like it.

Lets see where I can get in the the next month of spare time. 

I have a few idea's in mind from here still and I hope everyone has a great June!
